What to Expect

Your cosmetic dentistry journey

1

Cosmetic Consultation

During your initial consultation, we'll discuss your smile goals, examine your teeth, and take photos to assess your current dental situation. You'll have the opportunity to share what you'd like to change about your smile.

2

Treatment Planning

Based on your goals and dental needs, we'll develop a customized treatment plan. For complex cases, we may use digital smile design technology to show you a preview of your potential results.

3

Preparation Appointment

Depending on your treatment plan, you may need a preparation appointment. For veneers, this may involve minimal tooth preparation; for whitening, we might take impressions for custom trays.

4

Treatment Implementation

We'll perform your selected cosmetic procedure(s) with meticulous attention to detail. Some treatments like bonding may be completed in one visit, while others like veneers may require multiple appointments.

5

Follow-Up & Maintenance

After your treatment is complete, we'll schedule a follow-up appointment to ensure you're satisfied with the results and provide instructions for maintaining your beautiful new smile.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about cosmetic dentistry

The time required varies by procedure. Professional teeth whitening can be completed in a single 60-90 minute appointment. Dental bonding typically takes 30-60 minutes per tooth. Veneers usually require two appointments spaced about 2-3 weeks apart. A complete smile makeover may take several appointments over a few months, depending on the treatments involved.

Professional teeth whitening results can last from 6 months to 2 years, depending on your lifestyle habits. Factors that can stain teeth include consuming coffee, tea, red wine, and tobacco use. Regular maintenance and good oral hygiene can help extend the results. We also offer touch-up kits for at-home use to maintain your bright smile.

Porcelain veneers are considered a permanent treatment because a small amount of enamel is typically removed from the teeth to accommodate them. The veneers themselves can last 10-15 years or longer with proper care. When they eventually need replacement, new veneers will be created. With advances in technology, many cases now require minimal or no tooth preparation.

Purely cosmetic procedures are typically not covered by dental insurance. However, some treatments may be partially covered if they also serve a functional purpose. For example, a crown that improves both appearance and function might receive partial coverage. Our administrative team can help verify your insurance benefits and discuss financing options to make your smile transformation affordable.

Our dentists have extensive training in aesthetic dentistry and work closely with skilled dental laboratories to ensure your smile enhancement looks beautiful and natural. We'll take into account your facial features, skin tone, and existing teeth to create harmonious results.
