What to Do in a Dental Emergency

Important steps before you reach our office

For a Knocked-Out Tooth

  1. Pick up the tooth by the crown (not the root)
  2. Gently rinse it with water if dirty (do not scrub)
  3. If possible, place the tooth back in its socket
  4. If that's not possible, keep the tooth moist in milk, saliva, or a tooth preservation product
  5. Call us immediately — time is critical for saving a knocked-out tooth

For a Severe Toothache

  1. Rinse your mouth with warm water
  2. Use dental floss to remove any food caught between teeth
  3. Take over-the-counter pain relievers as directed
  4. Apply a cold compress to your cheek for swelling
  5. Call our office for same-day treatment

For a Chipped or Broken Tooth

  1. Rinse your mouth with warm water
  2. Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling
  3. Save any broken pieces if possible
  4. Cover any sharp edges with dental wax if available
  5. Contact us for prompt treatment

For a Dental Abscess

  1. Rinse with mild salt water several times a day
  2. Take over-the-counter pain medication as directed
  3. Apply a cold compress to your face for swelling
  4. Do not apply aspirin directly to the affected area
  5. Call us immediately — dental abscesses require prompt treatment

Important: If you experience severe swelling, uncontrolled bleeding, or trauma to the face or jaw, go to the nearest emergency room immediately or call 911.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about emergency dental care

Dental emergencies include situations that require immediate attention to relieve severe pain, stop bleeding, or save a tooth. These include knocked-out teeth, severely cracked or broken teeth, abscesses or infections with swelling, severe toothaches, lost fillings or crowns that cause pain, and injuries to the soft tissues of the mouth. If you're in severe pain or experiencing any of these issues, you should contact us right away.

At Wisdom Bites Dental Clinic, we prioritize emergency cases and make every effort to see patients experiencing dental emergencies on the same day they contact us. We reserve time in our daily schedule specifically for emergency appointments. When you call with an emergency, our team will ask questions to assess the severity of your situation and get you in as quickly as possible.

If your tooth is knocked out after our regular business hours, first call our emergency number, as we often have on-call staff for serious emergencies. While waiting to be seen: 1) Handle the tooth by the crown, not the root. 2) Gently rinse it with water if dirty, but don't scrub. 3) Try to reinsert it in the socket if possible. 4) If not, keep the tooth moist in milk, saliva, or a tooth preservation product. 5) Get to our office or an emergency dental provider as soon as possible—ideally within 30 minutes for the best chance of saving the tooth.

Most dental insurance plans provide coverage for emergency dental procedures, as they are considered necessary care. However, coverage varies by provider and specific plan. Our administrative team can help verify your insurance benefits and provide an estimate of your out-of-pocket costs. We also offer payment plans and financing options for emergency treatments not fully covered by insurance, as we believe financial concerns should never prevent you from getting the emergency care you need.

While not all dental emergencies can be prevented, many can be avoided through: 1) Regular dental check-ups to catch problems early. 2) Wearing a mouthguard during sports activities. 3) Avoiding chewing hard foods like ice or hard candy that can crack teeth. 4) Not using teeth as tools to open packages or bottles. 5) Maintaining good oral hygiene to prevent infections. 6) Addressing minor dental issues before they become major problems. Remember, preventive care is always better than emergency treatment.
