Services

What dental services do you offer?

We offer a comprehensive range of dental services including general dentistry (checkups, cleanings, fillings), cosmetic dentistry (whitening, veneers, bonding), emergency dental care, orthodontics, periodontics, and pediatric dentistry. Our team is equipped to handle all your dental needs in one convenient location.

Do you provide teeth whitening services?

Yes, we offer both in-office professional teeth whitening and take-home whitening kits. Our in-office treatment provides immediate results in just one visit, while our custom take-home trays allow you to whiten gradually at your convenience. During your consultation, we'll recommend the best option based on your specific needs and preferences.

How often should I get a dental checkup?

For most patients, we recommend a dental checkup and professional cleaning every six months. However, depending on your oral health status, we may suggest more frequent visits. Regular checkups are essential for preventing dental problems, detecting issues early, and maintaining optimal oral health.

Appointment durations vary based on the type of service:

When scheduling, our team will provide you with an estimated time frame for your specific procedure.

Dental Care

What's the best way to maintain oral health between visits?

To maintain excellent oral health between dental visits:

  • Brush your teeth twice daily with fluoride toothpaste
  • Floss daily to clean between teeth
  • Use an antimicrobial or fluoride mouthwash
  • Maintain a balanced diet low in sugary foods and drinks
  • Drink plenty of water throughout the day
  • Replace your toothbrush every 3-4 months
  • Avoid tobacco products

Following these practices will help prevent cavities, gum disease, and other oral health issues.

When should I bring my child for their first dental visit?

We recommend scheduling your child's first dental visit by their first birthday or within six months after their first tooth erupts. Early dental visits help establish a positive relationship with dental care, allow us to monitor development, and provide parents with guidance on proper oral hygiene techniques for children. These early visits are typically short and focus on making your child comfortable in the dental environment.

What should I do in a dental emergency?

In case of a dental emergency:

  1. Call our office immediately at (555) 123-4567. We reserve time in our schedule for emergency patients.
  2. For a knocked-out tooth, gently rinse it without scrubbing, try to reinsert it, or place it in milk and seek care immediately.
  3. For a cracked tooth, rinse with warm water and apply a cold compress to reduce swelling.
  4. For toothaches, rinse with warm water, use dental floss to remove any debris, and take over-the-counter pain relievers as directed.

Outside of business hours, our answering service will connect you with our on-call dentist for guidance.
